1. Executive Summary

We are building a focused, AI-first doctor portal for Udaipur, Rajasthan, designed to expand across India. Patients discover doctors, and the moment they want to connect, a popup form captures them as a lead; staff then convert that lead into a confirmed booking. It takes the most valuable, lowest-cost parts of Practo and Apollo247 — discovery, listings, lead capture, booking — and ships them fast, leaving heavier features (video consult, medicine, labs, payments) for later phases.

What v1 is

  • Doctor/clinic listings & onboarding
  • Patient discovery & AI smart search
  • Lead capture popup (the "call/book" flow)
  • Appointment booking + reminders
  • Role-based admin / manager / doctor / front-desk
  • 5 AI agents assisting throughout

What v1 is not (yet)

  • Video / audio / chat consultation
  • e-Prescriptions & online payments
  • Medicine delivery & lab tests
  • Full health-record vault, ABDM/ABHA

Architected so each can bolt on without a rewrite.

Why this approach wins

  • Cheap to launch: Vercel + Supabase run near-free at MVP volumes (see Ch. 12).
  • Fast to build: a two-agent workflow (Claude Code + Codex) on a proven stack.
  • Differentiated: AI search, lead assistance, triage and a staff copilot from day one.
  • Local first: winning Udaipur's supply (doctors) and demand (patients) before scaling.

The 3-phase roadmap

1 — MVPListing · Lead · BookingListings, search, lead popup, booking, RBAC, 2 AI agents (Smart Search + Lead-Form Assistant). Udaipur launch.
2 — EngageConvert & retainNotifications, reviews, full agent suite (triage, staff copilot, content/SEO), analytics, more cities.
3 — ExpandHealthcare super-appPayments, teleconsult, labs/medicine, health records, ABDM/ABHA.
Recommended LLM stack (answer to "which AI"): serve all models through Vercel AI Gateway and route by task — Claude Haiku 4.5 for cheap high-volume work, Claude Sonnet 4.6 as the reasoning default, Claude Opus 4.8 only for the hardest agentic flows, plus a small embedding model in Supabase pgvector for semantic search. Full detail in Chapter 6.

3. Product Vision & Scope

Vision: the fastest way for a patient in Udaipur to find the right doctor and get a confirmed appointment — and the easiest way for a doctor to receive qualified, well-summarized patients — with AI removing friction at every step.

Guiding principles

  • Mobile-first, low-end Android friendly, Hindi + English ready.
  • SEO-first public pages (ISR) — organic discovery is the cheapest growth.
  • Trust: verified doctors, moderated reviews, clear consent, medical disclaimers.

RLS mechanism: scoping columns (tenant_id, owner_doctor_id, clinic_id, assigned_manager_id, patient_id) are compared against auth.uid() and the user's role row in every policy. The AI service role uses scoped service credentials and writes an audit_log entry per action.

4.1 Multi-tenancy — a clinic/hospital is a tenant

The platform is multi-tenant (B2B2C): each clinic/hospital is a tenant whose staff and data are isolated from every other tenant. The Super Admin (platform) sits above all tenants; Admins/Managers/Doctors/Front-desk live inside one tenant; Patients are cross-tenant consumers scoped to their own records.

  • Every table carries tenant_id; every query and RLS policy is tenant-scoped — tenant isolation is mandatory, no shared data unless explicit.
  • Tenant context is validated server-side on every request; tenant access is audited.
  • Isolation is tested regularly; security before convenience. Shared infrastructure is fine, shared data is forbidden.

5. Core Feature Modules

5.1 Doctor onboarding & listing

Sign-up → build profile (name, specialties, qualifications, experience, languages, fees, clinic & location, hours, photos) → upload proof for verification/KYC → Admin approves → listing goes live (SEO page). Doctors manage their own staff and availability.

5.2 Discovery & search

Browse by specialty/area; filter by specialty, location, availability, gender, language, fee, rating. AI Smart Search turns free text ("child fever doctor near Fatehsagar, evening") into ranked matches using hybrid keyword + vector search and business rules.

5.3 Lead capture (the popup)

  • Triggered by Call / Book on any profile or search card.
  • Collects name, phone (OTP-verified), problem/symptoms, preferred time, consent.
  • AI Lead-Form Assistant validates & normalizes input, summarizes the problem, de-dupes against existing leads, and tags the likely specialty.
  • Creates a lead with source tracking (which doctor/page/campaign).
  • Sends instant SMS/WhatsApp confirmation; raises a realtime alert to staff.

5.4 Lead pipeline

Kanban/list of leads with status New → Contacted → Booked → Closed/Lost, assignment, notes, an SLA timer (time-to-first-contact), and full activity history (lead_events).

5.5 Appointment booking

Per-doctor slot calendar from availability rules; create/confirm/reschedule/cancel; double-booking prevention; automatic reminders; no-show tracking.

5.6 Dashboards & analytics

Role-scoped dashboards: lead funnel, conversion, SLA breaches, bookings, show-rate, top doctors/specialties, source attribution. Live via Supabase Realtime.

5.7 Notifications

SMS / WhatsApp / email for lead & booking confirmations and reminders, behind a provider adapter (MSG91 / Gupshup / Twilio) so we can swap vendors.

5.8 Reviews & ratings (P2)

Post-visit rating requests; moderation queue; verified-visit badge; feeds search ranking.

6. The AI Layer — Multiple Agents & LLM Choice

Everything AI runs through the Vercel AI Gateway (one API, provider fallbacks, observability, optional zero-data-retention — important for health data) built with the AI SDK. We tier models by task to keep cost low.

6.1 Which LLM to use (direct recommendation)

TierModelUse it forWhy
Cheap / high-volumeClaude Haiku 4.5lead parsing & validation, classification, summaries, first-pass triagefast & low-cost at scale
Reasoning (default)Claude Sonnet 4.6smart-search synthesis, staff copilot, content generationbest quality-per-rupee for real reasoning
Hardest onlyClaude Opus 4.8complex multi-step agentic flows, long-context analysistop capability, used sparingly
Embeddingssmall embedding model
(text-embedding-3-small or open BGE)		semantic vectors for doctor searchcheap; stored in Supabase pgvector
Bottom line: default to Claude via the AI Gateway, start most calls on Haiku, escalate to Sonnet when quality needs it, reserve Opus for the few hard cases. This single decision is the biggest lever on your AI bill.

6.2 The five agents

#AgentJobModelPhase
1Smart Search & Matchfree text → ranked doctors (vector + keyword + rules)Sonnet + embeddings1
2Lead-Form Assistantautofill, validate, transcribe, summarize, de-dupe leadsHaiku1
3Symptom Triagesymptoms → suggested specialty (triage, not diagnosis)Haiku→Sonnet2
4Staff Copilotlead summaries, suggested replies, follow-up nudges, daily reportsSonnet2
5Content & SEOdoctor bios, specialty pages, local SEO copy Sonnet2

6.3 Guardrails (all agents)

  • Run under the AI service role; every action audit-logged (ai_runs).
  • PII redaction before model calls where possible; honour DPDP consent.
  • Token + rate limits and a cost ceiling per tenant.
  • Tools are RLS-scoped — an agent only sees what its caller's role can see.
  • Medical safety: triage shows a clear disclaimer, never diagnoses, and routes red-flag symptoms (chest pain, stroke signs, breathing difficulty) straight to emergency guidance.
Compliance note: the symptom agent is a triage helper, not a medical device. Keep the disclaimer visible and log consent.

14.3 The markdown memory system "save every chat & contact"

  • docs/DECISION_LOG.md — append-only; every decision with date, what, why, who. Never delete an entry. This is the project's long-term memory.
  • docs/HANDOVER.md — the living "current state": what was just done, what's next, open questions. Overwrite the top each session; append to History.
  • Contacts & key facts — captured in the relevant doc (e.g. provider contacts in ARCHITECTURE.md), and important cross-session facts saved to the assistant's memory so they survive new chats.

14.4 Versioning & backup "keep old version before new"

Before starting any new version, snapshot the current working tree into .versions/vN/ (the version you are leaving), tag the git commit, and add a row to the version table in VERSIONING.md. Snapshots are read-only and never deleted.

16. Delivery Workflow & Traceability

Every feature flows through one pipeline. Documentation, backups and handovers are never skipped. Work proceeds in small modular steps — Plan → Build → Test → Log → Repeat.

Requirements ─► Architecture ─► Database ─► Backend ─► Frontend ─► Testing ─► Documentation ─► Deployment
   PM            Architect        DB          Backend     Frontend      QA           Docs            DevOps

Every feature must update (no exceptions)

Docs
  • PRD.md — the requirement
  • CHANGELOG.md — what changed
  • USER_FLOWS.md — affected flows
  • API docs — endpoints & contracts
  • dev-log.md, tasks.md — progress
Code & safety
  • migration file for any schema change
  • tests with every feature (QA gate)
  • backup/snapshot before major change
  • handover note after every task
  • DECISION_LOG.md if a decision was made

Traceability — requirement → deployment

REQ-id (PRD) ─► ADR (architecture.md) ─► migration ─► API endpoint ─► UI flow
            ─► test case ─► CHANGELOG entry ─► deploy (Vercel) ─► handover note

Any shipped line of code can be traced back to a requirement, and any requirement traced forward to its deployment. Correctness before speed; security before features.

Hard gates: no release without tests passing · no schema change without a migration · no decision overwrite without a DECISION_LOG.md update · no major change without a .versions/ backup.

18. Governance Rulebook (full)

The complete operating ruleset for this and future projects. Mirrored to /docs/GOVERNANCE.md for the agents. Condensed for print; each line is a rule.

Core Principles
Security before features · correctness before speed · simplicity before complexity · scalability before shortcuts · maintainability before hacks · reuse before duplication · reliability before optimization · automation before manual · UX before visual effects · data integrity is non-negotiable.
Architecture
Clean architecture · separate UI / logic / data · no business logic in UI · loosely coupled, highly cohesive · design for scale · domain-driven · event-driven where useful · avoid premature microservices · start modular, scale when needed.
Code Quality
No duplication · no dead code · no unused deps · no magic values · meaningful names · small focused functions · single responsibility · maintainable files · readability over cleverness · every change improves the codebase.
TypeScript
Strict mode · never any · explicit types · consistent interfaces/types · validate external data · no unsafe casts · shared FE/BE types · strong typing for APIs, DB models, everywhere.
Database
DB is source of truth · migrations only · no manual prod edits · timestamps on every record · audit logs on critical actions · never trust client data · proper indexes · optimized queries · protect integrity · design for growth.
API
Validate every request & response · never trust FE input · consistent response format · proper error handling · authn + authz · rate-limit sensitive endpoints · log failures · version breaking APIs.
Security
Deny by default · least privilege · encrypt sensitive data · protect secrets · never expose keys · server-side authz only · audit privileged actions · monitor anomalies · MFA where applicable · security review before release.
Authentication
Trusted providers (Supabase Auth) · no manual password storage · secure sessions · role-based perms · session expiry · audit auth events · prevent privilege escalation · secure reset · verify identity for sensitive actions.
Authorization
Backend enforces perms · FE visibility ≠ security · role checks on every action · resource- & tenant-level perms · admin actions logged · sensitive actions confirmed · no implicit trust · least privilege.
Multi-tenancy
Tenant isolation mandatory · no data leakage · every query scoped · tenant context validated · audit tenant access · per-tenant perms · shared infra ok, shared data forbidden unless explicit · isolation tested regularly.
Frontend
Fast loading · responsive · accessible · consistent reusable components · proper loading/error states · safe optimistic updates · offline when required · UX first.
Backend
Stateless where possible · centralized business logic · reliable error handling · structured logging · idempotent ops · background jobs for heavy work · secure endpoints · scalable services · observability.
AI
Never the source of truth · output verifiable · human override always · log decisions, prompts & outputs · protect user data · avoid hallucination · validate responses · safeguards on actions · fail safely.
File Storage
Object storage · metadata separate · validate uploads · restrict access · signed URLs · encrypt sensitive files · scan when possible · CDN delivery · backup critical assets · lifecycle management.
Realtime
DB remains truth · realtime is delivery only · handle reconnects & conflicts · idempotent updates · tenant-scoped channels · monitor latency · recover gracefully · sync after reconnect.
Offline
Queue writes · auto-sync · safe conflict resolution · show sync status · protect integrity · visible failures · minimal local data · encrypt local sensitive data · audit sync.
Performance
Measure before optimizing · cache intelligently · lazy-load heavy content · minimize DB load · optimize requests & assets · monitor continuously · reduce bundle size · CDN · user-perceived speed first.
Testing
Test critical workflows, business logic, security, permissions, integrations, edge cases & failures · automate · prevent regressions · no release without validation.
DevOps
Infra as code · automated deploys & backups · monitoring + alerts · disaster recovery · rollback capability · environment separation · secret management · operational visibility.
Compliance
Minimize data · protect PII · audit trails · appropriate retention · support deletion · track consent · encrypt sensitive info · follow regional regs (DPDP) · log compliance actions · regular reviews.
Product
Solve real problems · user value · avoid bloat · measure adoption & outcomes · collect feedback · iterate · prioritize reliability & retention · build for long-term sustainability.
Golden Rules
Security first · data is sacred · users are the priority · simplicity wins · reliability · scalability · maintainability · observability · automation · long-term thinking beats short-term shortcuts.
